About the Macro Regime Score

The MRS is a monthly composite indicator of the U.S. macro-financial regime, built from 13 indicators across five pillars — Growth (30%), Credit (20%), Market Stress (20%), Inflation (15%) and Liquidity/Rates (15%). Each indicator is converted to an expanding-window z-score (minimum 24-month window, clipped to ±3); the composite is the weighted sum of pillar scores, shown on a 1–5 display scale (3 + composite z, clipped to [1, 5]).

Regimes — Expansion, Neutral, Slowdown, Contraction — are assigned from fixed composite-z thresholds (+0.35 / −0.30 / −1.00) with a two-month confirmation rule before a regime change is considered confirmed.

This dashboard reads exclusively from Research/MRS/MRS_Master.xlsx, produced by Src/mrs_monitoring_store.py from the canonical monitoring tables (methodology Part VII) and exported to JSON by Src/export_dashboard_data.py. No values are computed in the browser — the only authored content is the monthly analyst note.
